The next step is a billing and account review by Support—here’s what to expect and how to proceed based on GitHub Docs.

I understand you left the third‑party organization and want to resume a personal Copilot Pro subscription. Changes to Copilot access that involve restrictions, organization seat assignments, or account eligibility require a manual account review from GitHub Trust & Safety / Support and may involve billing/seat state that only Support can change.

What you should expect and next steps (grounded in GitHub Docs):

Why Support review is required

If your personal Copilot access was disabled because you were assigned a seat through an organization or because of a policy violation, only GitHub Trust & Safety / Support can review account history and lift restrictions. Documentation for managing Copilot at the org level explains that personal subscriptions are affected when a seat is assigned or when policy constraints exist; reversing restrictions that arose from those actions requires intervention by Support. See Viewing and changing your Copilot plan (opens in a new tab) and the organization admin docs linked below for context.
What Support will check (based on docs and standard flow)

whether your account is still attached to any organization Copilot seat
any active restrictions or policy flags that caused Copilot access to be disabled
whether your personal Copilot subscription was automatically canceled when assigned an org seat (Docs: Viewing and changing your Copilot plan (opens in a new tab))
If you want to subscribe again after Support clears the restriction

Once any restriction is lifted and there’s no active organization seat blocking your account, you can subscribe to Copilot individually from your account settings: Settings → Billing & licensing (or Plans and usage) and then the GitHub Copilot section to subscribe or upgrade. See Getting started with a GitHub Copilot plan (opens in a new tab) for sign‑up steps.
If the organization still shows as granting you access

Organization owners can revoke seats or an owner can disable Copilot Business for the org. For org owners the docs show how to revoke or remove seats: Revoking access to GitHub Copilot for members of your organization (opens in a new tab) and Canceling GitHub Copilot for your organization (opens in a new tab). Billing for seats is affected by when seats are removed (takes effect at start of next billing cycle).
If the steps above don’t resolve the restriction, please continue with this support request so Trust & Safety can review your account history and lift the restriction if appropriate. Because this is a billing and enforcement action, GitHub Support / Trust & Safety are the only teams that can complete the account review and re‑enable your ability to purchase Copilot Pro.
